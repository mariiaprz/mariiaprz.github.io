Who is Judeline

Born Lara Fernández Castrelo on January 18, 2003, in Jerez de la Frontera, Judeline spent her childhood in the luminous coastal village of Los Caños de Meca (Cádiz) , immersed in the salt-air ambience of Andalusia, where music, light and tradition were ever-present. Surrounded by flamenco echoes and seaside winds, she learned to hum before she could speak—and from an early age she carried a profound connection to sound and storytelling that continues to shape her music today.

Her stage name—Judeline—is a playful, heartfelt tribute to The Beatles’ “Hey Jude”, a song her father, himself a musician of Venezuelan origin, often played in their home. What began in the digital world—covers posted online and early experiments in sound—quickly transformed into a fiercely personal, genre-defying identity. She blends the emotional weight of flamenco with the fluid dynamics of R&B, trap and electronic textures, creating a soundscape that feels both ancient and futuristic.

“Mi música es una fusión de todo lo que me ha llegado desde pequeña: el flamenco que se oía en mi casa, el reggae y la electrónica en los bares de Caños de Meca, y el R&B de mis hermanos."

At seventeen she made the leap to Madrid, drawn by its creative intensity and willing to abandon the familiar for the unknown. There she immersed herself in jam sessions and studio nights, assimilating the pulse of the city while never relinquishing her southern roots. Her first solo release, the EP de la luz (2022), introduced her vision: a luminous, genre-stretching work rooted in tradition but reaching for tomorrow.

With the 2024 release of Bodhiria, Judeline delivered her most ambitious statement yet. The title—derived from the Sanskrit word “bodhi”, meaning enlightenment —reflects the album’s themes of self-discovery, resilience and transcendence. In the guise of her alter-ego Angel-a she guides listeners through a metaphysical journey of love, identity and connection. The songs weave expressive melodies, rich rhythms and ethereal electronic soundscapes, while the visuals merge Andalusian heritage, Venezuelan legacy and futuristic aesthetics. Bodhiria secured her reputation as a groundbreaking artist and critics and fans alike took notice.

Her live presence is equally compelling. From the stages of Primavera Sound, Sónar Festival and Mad Cool Festival to an unforgettable debut at Coachella Festival, she brought her Andalusian soul and electronic warmth to audiences around the world.

Alongside her artistic evolution, Judeline has earned accolades and recognition, including being named an Apple Music Up Next Spain artist and a Vevo DSCVR Artist to Watch. In 2025 she claimed both the MME Award and the Public Choice Award at Eurosonic Noorderslag (ESNS). But beyond the trophies, she remains committed to integrity, truth and creative freedom.

Today, Judeline stands at a crossroads of tradition and innovation—a voice from the south that speaks to the global, a sound rooted in her heritage yet unafraid of tomorrow. She doesn’t just perform music: she invites you into a world where memory, light and rhythm converge.

The Sound Journey

de la luz (2022) — The Beginning of a Voice

Her debut EP, de la luz, marked the emergence of Judeline’s unique sonic identity. Created with long-time collaborators Tuiste and Mayo, the project introduced her ability to balance intimacy and experimentation. Across its tracks, she intertwines subtle electronic production with ethereal vocals and poetic lyricism, exploring themes of growth, clarity, and emotional light.

The EP was widely praised by critics and fellow artists for its innovative yet soulful approach — Rosalía and Bad Bunny both highlighted Judeline as one of the new voices redefining Spanish pop. de la luz isn’t just a debut; it’s a declaration of intent — a reflection of how she sees music as illumination, guiding her evolution toward deeper creative worlds.

Bodhiria (2024) — Enlightenment Through Sound

If de la luz was the dawn, Bodhiria is the awakening. Co-produced by Rob Bisel (SZA, Tate McRae), Mayo, Tuiste, and Drummie, the album expands Judeline’s universe into a layered, cinematic experience. The title, drawn from the Sanskrit “bodhi” (meaning enlightenment), reflects a journey through identity, transformation, and emotional transcendence.

The record introduces her alter ego Angel-a, a spiritual counterpart who guides listeners through an introspective narrative where love, loss, and rebirth coexist. Bodhiria is a work that feels both ancestral and hypermodern. Praised by Pitchfork, Rolling Stone, and Billboard, it cemented Judeline as one of the most forward-thinking voices.
